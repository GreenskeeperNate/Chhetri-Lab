Research

What we work on

Our research connects turfgrass stress physiology, plant growth regulators, and on course management. Projects are designed with superintendents and sports turf managers in mind.

Area 1

Poa annua seedhead control programs

We evaluate PGR programs and supporting products for annual bluegrass seedhead suppression, with a focus on mixed Poa and creeping bentgrass putting greens and fairways.

  • Comparing two versus three application programs across fall and spring.
  • Testing ethephon, trinexapac ethyl, Civitas, and their combinations.
  • Assessing seedhead cover, turf quality, surface smoothness, and playability.

Area 2

Turfgrass under environmental stress

Dr. Chhetri has a strong background in shade and drought stress, including work on turf type bermudagrass under combined stress and performance under reduced light.

  • Bermudagrass performance in low light environments.
  • Combined shade and drought effects on turf quality and persistence.
  • Traffic, temperature, and other stresses that affect golf and sports turf.

Area 3

Practical tools and decision support

Long term, we aim to turn research into simple programs, timing guides, and scouting workflows that fit within busy maintenance schedules.

  • Calendar and physiology based timing schemes for PGRs.
  • Visual rating systems tied to seedhead cover and quality thresholds.
  • Optional use of NDVI meters or photo ratings where they provide extra value.

Current project

Improving Poa annua seedhead control programs

This M.S. project evaluates plant growth regulator programs for annual bluegrass seedhead suppression on mixed Poa and creeping bentgrass greens and fairways at University Park.

Objectives

  • Compare two versus three application programs that include a late fall timing and spring timings.
  • Test the effect of Civitas Turf Defense when combined with ethephon and trinexapac ethyl.
  • Measure total non structural carbohydrates in storage tissues to see how programs affect plant energy.
  • Assess changes in root distribution and health in response to PGR programs.
Treatments combine timing programs (fall and spring) with PGR combinations, plus untreated controls.

Experimental approach

  • Randomized complete block design on a mixed stand green and fairway.
  • Nine total treatments that compare timing programs and PGR combinations.
  • Weekly ratings for seedhead cover, suppression, and visual turf quality during the spring flush.
  • Additional data such as canopy density, NDVI, moisture, firmness, and temperature where practical.
  • Laboratory measurements of total non structural carbohydrates and root distribution on selected dates.

Results will be summarized into clear program recommendations that help superintendents decide when to use two versus three applications and when to include Civitas in Poa seedhead programs.

Facilities and opportunities

Where we work and how to get involved

Our research combines on site field trials at the Valentine Turfgrass Research Center with greenhouse and laboratory work through the Center for Turfgrass Science.

Field and lab facilities

  • Valentine Turfgrass Research Center with research greens, fairways, and sports turf surfaces.
  • Mixed species greens and fairways used for Poa seedhead and PGR trials.
  • Access to teaching and research labs in the Agricultural Industries Building and related facilities.

These facilities support work on seedhead control, turf stress management, and decision tools that match real maintenance conditions.

Student and industry opportunities

  • Graduate study in Turfgrass Management and related programs.
  • Undergraduate research assistant positions during the growing season.
  • On course collaborations with golf courses and sports facilities.

Prospective students can email Dr. Chhetri with a CV, a short statement of interest, and any turf industry or research experience. Industry partners can contact the lab with basic information about their facility and the type of project they have in mind.
